Events & Education

Connect, Learn, and Support the Herd

The Northwest Camelid Foundation hosts annual fundraiser events and educational programs that bring camelid owners and veterinarians together across the Pacific Northwest. All proceeds fund veterinary research and scholarships.

Annual Fundraiser Events

Each year, NWCF hosts fundraiser events that are the primary source of funding for our research grants and scholarships. These events are open to all camelid owners, breeders, veterinarians, and anyone who cares about the health and well-being of llamas and alpacas.

Our fundraisers have included silent auctions featuring fiber arts, camelid equipment, veterinary products, and experiences donated by our generous community of supporters. All proceeds go directly to camelid veterinary research at OSU and WSU and to the maintenance of the OSU research herd.

"Every dollar raised at our events goes directly to research. There is no overhead, no administrative cost — just science."

— Northwest Camelid Foundation

What to Expect

  • Silent auction with donated items from breeders, vendors, and community supporters
  • Camelid demonstrations and showcase animals
  • Updates from OSU and WSU researchers on current funded studies
  • Networking with veterinarians and camelid owners from across the region
  • Opportunity to ask questions directly of the Research Committee

Educational Programming

Meet & Greet Events

Get your camelid health questions answered by experienced veterinarians from OSU and WSU in a relaxed, accessible format.

Community Involvement

Industry Conferences & Shows

NWCF members and board representatives regularly attend regional and national camelid shows and conferences, including events hosted by the Alpaca Owners Association, the Llama Association of North America, and state-level organizations across the Pacific Northwest.

These events are an opportunity to distribute educational materials, share information about our research funding, and connect with supporters who share our commitment to camelid health.

If you are involved with a regional camelid organization and would like to partner with NWCF on an educational event or program, we would love to hear from you.
