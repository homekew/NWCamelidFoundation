Camelid Fun Under the Trees: NWCF's 2024 Fundraiser

September 13, 2024  ·  Events & Fundraisers

Llama and alpaca enthusiasts gathered in central Oregon in August for NWCF's 2024 fundraiser event, hosted by Crescent Moon Ranch. Lunch was provided by Bleu Bite Catering, sponsored by Glen Pfefferkorn.

As always, 100% of the funds raised went directly to camelid research. The event brought together owners from across the Pacific Northwest for a day of community, good food, and a shared commitment to the health of their herds.

Thank you to everyone who attended, donated, and helped make it a success. Details about the 2025 fundraiser will be announced to our email list first — sign up on our events page to be notified.

A Message From Dean Susan Tornquist

Message from Leadership

Dean Tornquist recently attended the annual Northwest Camelid Foundation fundraiser at a farm near Wilsonville, Oregon, and was honored to give remarks about the long-standing relationship between the NWCF and OSU's Carlson College of Veterinary Medicine.

Her message reflected on decades of partnership — and on what that partnership has meant for camelid health research, veterinary education, and the communities of llama and alpaca owners throughout the Pacific Northwest.

A full transcript of Dean Tornquist's remarks is available by contacting us at info@NWCamelidFoundation.org.

Every Herd Benefits from NWCF-Funded Research

Foundation Update

Regardless of the size of their herd or the location of their barn, every camelid and every owner benefits from the research funded by the NWCF.

From its beginning in 1987, the Foundation's mission has grown into a concerted effort to support camelid medical research at Oregon State University's College of Veterinary Medicine and to provide funds to maintain the herd of llamas and alpacas available to OSU researchers.

The NWCF is proud to have played a role in the outstanding reputation OSU has built over the years for camelid research — and in the valuable opportunity the university provides for veterinary students to receive hands-on camelid education.
